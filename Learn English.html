Part Of Speech

The following words are the division of part of speech in English Language
  1. NOUNS:A noun is a name of a person,animal,place,or things(idea or concept).Example:Kunle,Dog,Country,Book
  2. PRONOUN:A pronoun is a word whichtaje place(or is use instead) of a noun.Exampe:i,me,she,he,you,they,etc.
  3. VERB:A verb is a doing or action word.Example:go,come,jump,swim,dance,etc
  4. ADVERB:An adverb modifies(or qualifies) a verb.Example:lovely,catefully,plesantlly,happyly,etc
  5. ADJECTIVE:AN adjective qualifies a noun or a pronoun.Example:tall,short,old,new,long,etc
  6. PREPOSITION:A preposition shows the relationship between one thing and the other.It usually come before a noun or a pronoun.Example:at,in,on,under,beside,etc
  7. CONJUNCTION:A conjunction is a word used join word together.Example:and,when,yet,etc
  8. INTERJECTION(EXCLAMATION):An interjection express sudden and emotions.Eaxmple:Ah!,Stop!,Shut the door!,etc
  9. ARTICLE:An article isva kind if adjetive which is always used with and gives some information about a noun. There are two articles 'A and THE',but they are used very often and are important for using English accurtely.Eample: The wheel?,A cat was chasing a mouse in my house,etc.

Question And Answer Tags

There are some statement that need to be confirmed.These statement are known as QUESTION TAGS,and the answers use for the confirmation are known as ANSWER TAGS.

The following are the principles that guide Question and Answer Tags.

I.A negative statement requires a positive question tag.
Example:
You don't like playing football;Do you?
The answer tag can be Yes or No depending on the situation.For the statement above the answer can be:
Yes,i do (if he like playing football), or
No,i don't.(if he dosen't like playing football)

II.A positive statement requires a negative question tag
Example:
You enjoy listening to music;don't you
The answer tag can be:
Yes,i do(when the person enjoy listening to music)
No,i didn't(when the person not enjoy listening to music).

III.The tense of the question and answer tag must agree to the tense of the statement. Example:
We shall go tommorrow.Shall we?
The question tag follows the statement above.You can see that the tense used for the statement agrees to the tense used for the question tag.They are all simple future tense.It would be wrong if the question tag should have been,'Shouldn't' we? since 'Shouldn't' is the past tense of shall.
